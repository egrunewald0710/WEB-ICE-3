WELCOME TO DUBAI

Early History

Dubai's history can be traced back to as early as 3000 BCE when it was settled by nomadic Bedouin tribes who thrived on fishing, herding, and trading. These early inhabitants sought refuge in the area due to its advantageous location along the Persian Gulf, providing access to both land and sea trade routes.

Traders and Pearling

In the 18th century, Dubai began to flourish as a trading port, particularly for those traveling between Mesopotamia and the Indian subcontinent. The town's natural harbor and its strategic position along the trade routes led to the growth of a bustling marketplace. During this period, pearl diving also became a significant economic activity, with Dubai emerging as a key player in the regional pearl trade.

Modern Dubai

Today, Dubai is a bustling metropolis known for its iconic skyline, luxurious lifestyle, and global appeal. It's a city of superlatives, housing some of the world's tallest buildings, including the Burj Khalifa, and the busiest international airport, Dubai International Airport. It is also famous for its shopping festivals, cultural events, and a diverse population, with people from all around the world calling Dubai their home.
